During the proposal presentations, the lecturer we presented to suggested that we could "beef up" the project a little. 
How to do that depends on the interpretation of the app.

**Option 1**:
Making the app be able to communicate on the local network with other phones that also have this app installed, so that everyone locally is on the same page. 
There's no distinct role for sysadmins, as the app can't actually do anything about the devices, but the communication could be a use case. 
Note that this would need a toggle, as it mightn't be a good idea in a public network (e.g. the person installing the suspicious device marks it as safe).

**Option 2**:
Making the app more focused on detecting network anomalies and suspicious devices within any network.
This is more useful for users that travel around, as there are probably better solutions for single households/offices anyways.

We needed to decide which of these to include in order to put the details into the functional specification.
The supervisor replied that it is up to us to decide which to do, but that both could be included in the functional spec and marked as desirable if time allows.
